What to check before you buy

  • Confirm the exact material grade, thickness or density required by the BOQ.
  • Ask for data sheets and, where relevant, fire/safety classifications.
  • Match accessories: trims, skirting, adhesives, screws and anchors.
  • Consider acoustic needs (underlay) and moisture exposure in each area.
  • Plan wastage (5–10%) based on room layout and cutting patterns.

Use cases

Common use cases include residential apartments, retail, offices, schools and portacabin builds. For heavy‑traffic areas choose higher wear layers or denser boards; for wet zones consider waterproof options like PS skirting or cement boards.
